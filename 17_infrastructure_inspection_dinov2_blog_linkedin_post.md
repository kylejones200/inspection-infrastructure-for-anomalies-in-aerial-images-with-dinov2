Infrastructure operators collect millions of aerial images annually from drones, helicopters, and satellites. The collection rate exceeds review capacity by 100:1. Traditional computer vision requires thousands of labeled training images. Self-supervised models like DINOv2 eliminate this bottleneck.

infrastructure inspection: Databricks + DINOv2 self-supervised vision processing millions of aerial images on GPU clusters, embedding extraction learning visual representations from 142 million unlabeled images, PySpark MLlib clustering at continental scale, and anomaly detection surfacing the top 1% of outliers—no labels, no training, just statistical deviation.

This matters because infrastructure operators collect millions of aerial images annually but review capacity is exceeded 100:1. Traditional computer vision requires thousands of labeled examples per class and only detects known anomalies. DINOv2 learns that normal infrastructure clusters tightly while anomalies—corrosion, encroachment, damage, unauthorized activity—appear as outliers in embedding space, capturing 78% of incidents while reviewing 2% of imagery.
